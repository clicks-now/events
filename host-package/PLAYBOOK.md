# Clicks Host Playbook

*Everything you need to know to run a Clicks event in your city.*

---

## What is Clicks?

Clicks is a city-wide partner meetup series for the Shopify ecosystem. Agencies, app developers, operators, and the people who work with them — in the same room, on purpose, twice a year.

Same format, every city. 2 hours, hard out.

No panels. No keynotes. No sponsor pitches. Just speed rounds and real conversation.

---

## What hosting actually involves

You don't need a big team, a budget, or a production company. You need:

- A venue that fits 40–80 people
- One co-host partner from the Shopify ecosystem
- 3–4 speakers willing to do 5 minutes each
- A few hours of planning over 4–6 weeks

That's it. Clicks handles the brand, the format, and the playbook. You bring the room.

## The format

| Time | What happens |
|------|-------------|
| 0:00 | Doors open. Bar open. People arrive. |
| 0:30 | Welcome + housekeeping (5 min) |
| 0:35 | Speed rounds — 3–4 speakers, 5 min each, hard cut |
| 1:05 | Break + open networking |
| 1:25 | Open floor (optional — skip if networking energy is high) |
| 1:50 | Closing remarks |
| 2:00 | Hard out |

**The hard out is non-negotiable.** It signals respect for people's time and keeps the format tight. Say it in your welcome. Mean it.

## Step-by-step: from idea to event

### Step 1 — Express interest
Fill out the host form at [clicks.now/host](https://clicks.now/host) or open an issue in the [events repo](https://github.com/clicks-now/events). Takes 5 minutes.

### Step 2 — Get approved
The Clicks team reviews your request and gets back to you within a few days. If it's a fit, you'll get an approval email with your marketing pack and next steps.

### Step 3 — Lock the basics (weeks 1–2)
- Confirm your venue
- Confirm your co-host partner
- Agree on a date

### Step 4 — Confirm speakers (weeks 2–3)
Reach out to 5–6 people to get 3–4 confirmed. Use the speaker invite template in your marketing pack. Brief them: 5 minutes, no deck required, no pitch, just tell us what you're working on.

### Step 5 — Promote (weeks 3–5)
Use the social copy in your marketing pack. Post on LinkedIn. Have your co-host post too. Drop it in relevant Slack groups and Discord channels. Add it to Luma or a simple event page if you want RSVPs.

### Step 6 — Prep for the day (week 6)
- Review the [run-of-show](./run-of-show.md) and fill in the blanks
- Brief your speakers: 5 minutes, I will cut you, no hard feelings
- Confirm AV with the venue
- Arrange someone for the door

### Step 7 — Run the event
Show up 45 minutes early. Test AV. Get a drink. Run the room like you own it (you do).

See [run-of-show.md](./run-of-show.md) for the full facilitator script.

### Step 8 — After the event
- Send thank-yous to speakers and co-host
- Post photos to the Clicks Discord
- Submit your event feedback within a week (template in `templates/event-feedback.md`)

## Venue guidelines

**Size:** 40–80 people. Standing room or mixed seating works better than lecture-style rows.

**Location:** Central. Easy transit access. If people have to drive 30 minutes and find parking, you've already lost half the room.

**AV:** A screen and HDMI input is all you need. Handheld mic is a nice-to-have for rooms over 50. Test it before anyone arrives.

**Drinks:** A bar or drinks service is part of the vibe. Sponsors can cover costs — if you have one, acknowledge them verbally at open and close. No logos on the stage.

**Timing:** Book the space for 3 hours. That gives you setup time before and a buffer after the hard out for stragglers.

---

## Speaker guidelines

You're recruiting people to do 5-minute speed rounds. Here's what to tell them:

- **Format:** 5 minutes, hard cut, no deck required
- **Topic:** What you're building, what's working, what broke — pick one thread and pull it
- **Audience:** Shopify ecosystem peers — agencies, apps, operators
- **No pitches.** Seriously. The room knows when it's being sold to.

Mix it up if you can — one agency, one app developer, one operator or brand. Diversity of perspective is what makes the speed rounds interesting.

## Co-host guidelines

Your co-host is a partner, not a sponsor.

- They co-brand the event alongside Clicks
- They help promote to their own network
- They split logistics with you (or take some off your plate)
- They do **not** control the agenda, the speakers, or the format
- The event is always **Clicks [City]** — not "[Co-host] presents Clicks"

Choose someone whose network complements yours. Two agencies isn't ideal. An agency + an app partner is better.

## Branding rules

- Use **Clicks [City]** as the event name (e.g. Clicks Toronto)
- Co-host logo appears alongside the Clicks wordmark — equal weight, not dominant
- No sponsor logos on stage, podium, or behind speakers
- No slide decks with logos or ads during the event itself
- Sponsors get a verbal mention at open and close only

## After the event: event feedback

Within a week of your event, submit an event feedback PR to the events repo. Use the template at `templates/event-feedback.md`.

This isn't bureaucracy. It's the thing that makes the next host's event better. Be honest about what didn't work. Be specific about what did. The person running Clicks [Your City] in 12 months is going to read this.

## FAQ

**Do I need a budget?**
Not necessarily. A venue that provides drinks as part of a booking deal covers most of the cost. If you want more — catering, a photographer, printed name tags — that's on you to fund. Sponsors can help but are not required.

**Can I charge for tickets?**
No. Clicks events are free to attend.

**Can I run Clicks in a city that already has an event?**
Each city runs its own events on its own schedule. If Toronto is already running, you can still run a second Toronto event — just coordinate with the existing host so you're not competing for the same room on the same night.

**What if my event has low turnout?**
It happens. 25 people in the right room beats 80 people who didn't want to be there. Run the format anyway. Submit the event feedback honestly. The next one will be bigger.

**What if I need to cancel?**
Notify the Clicks team as soon as possible. Remove the event listing. If people have RSVPs, let them know directly. Shit happens — just be upfront.

**Who do I contact if I need help?**
Discord → [clicks-now community](https://discord.gg/beRe7aD7Tw). Someone will get back to you.
